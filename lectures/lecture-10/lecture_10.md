# Lecture 10

The Ricardo model says nothing about inequality

- All workers get paid the same

## Heckscher-Ohlin model

- Endowment-based comparative advantage
  - Depends on the ratio of college/non-college of one country vs. another
  - And on the ratio of skill/non-skill based industries

## Stopler-Samuelson effect

- Price of skill-based products rises with free trade
- Demand for skilled labor rises in the skilled country
- Relative wage for skilled workers increase
- Inequality rises in the country with a larger skill ratio and decreases in the other country

---

Skill-biased technical change can increase the demand for skilled labor

- The tech is a complement for some skills and a substitute for other skills
- Models predict both trade and tech incrase inequality

## Job polarization

- Decline in middle-wage jobs due to automation and offshoring
