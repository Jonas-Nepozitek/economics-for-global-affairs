## Lucas critique

- "it is naive to predict the effects of a change in economic policy entirely based on historical data"
- Changes in policy will affect other things that are not covered by the model that we don't expect
  - We must be careful making policy recommendations from the gravity and other models

## Calculating cost of trading

- unit cost to produce at country i: $w_i$ (wages) / $A_i$ (productivity)
- $p_{in}=\frac{w_n}{A_n}d_{in}$
  - $d_{in}$: trade costs from i to n

Of my total expenditeu, what is the fract of those goods that country i buys from country n

- we spend goods in economy, what is the fractional that we spend on tradeable goods and from where

$\sum^N_{K=1}(p_{iK}^{-\epsilon})$

$\lambda_{in}=\frac{(p_{in})^{-\epsilon}}{\sum_K(p_{iK})^{-\epsilon}}$

- The denominator includes trading with everyone including yourself (autarky)

$P_i=(\sum^N_{K=1}(p_{ik})^{-\epsilon})^{\frac{-1}{\epsilon}}$

$\lambda_{in}=\frac{\frac{W_n}{A_n}d_{in}}{P_i}$

$\lambda_{ii}=(\frac{W_i}{P_i}\frac{1}{A_i})^{-\epsilon}$

$\frac{W_i}{P_i}=A_i(\lambda_{ii})^{-\frac{1}{\epsilon}}$ -> real GDP pc., where A is productivity and the second terms is gains from trade

- Smaller countries gain more from trade than larger ones
